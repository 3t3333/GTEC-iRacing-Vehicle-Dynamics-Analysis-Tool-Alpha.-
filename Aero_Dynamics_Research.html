Vehicle Dynamics: Empirical Aerodynamic Mapping

This document outlines the mathematical foundation for calculating dynamic aerodynamic balance and downforce distribution using iRacing telemetry data (.ibt).

1. The Core Aerodynamic Equation

Aerodynamic force (Downforce or Lift) is proportional to the square of the vehicle's airspeed. To model this empirically from telemetry, we use:

\[F_{aero} = \frac{1}{2} \rho v^2 A C_L\]

Where:

  • \(\rho\): Air density (kg/m³)
  • \(v\): Airspeed (m/s)
  • \(A\): Frontal area (m²)
  • \(C_L\): Coefficient of Lift/Downforce (dimensionless)

2. Isolating Aerodynamic Load from Suspension

To find the downforce distribution, we must isolate the aero-only component of the total vertical load measured at the wheels.

\[F_{aero\_axle} = F_{total\_axle} - F_{static\_axle} - F_{weight\_transfer}\]

Where longitudinal weight transfer is calculated using G-force and Center of Gravity height:

\[F_{weight\_transfer} = \frac{Mass \cdot G_{long} \cdot h_{cg}}{Wheelbase}\]

3. The Coast-Down Calibration Method

To automate calibration in the CLI, the algorithm identifies "Coast-Down" zones—moments where G-forces are zero but speed is high. In these zones:

\[\text{If } G_{long} \approx 0 \text{ and } G_{lat} \approx 0 \implies F_{aero} \approx F_{total} - F_{static}\]

The CLI can then solve for \(C_L\) using linear regression over various speed samples.

4. Aerodynamic Balance (% Front)

This determines the car's stability. As rake angle increases, the center of pressure moves forward, increasing this percentage.

\[AB = \frac{F_{aero\_front}}{F_{aero\_front} + F_{aero\_rear}} \cdot 100\%\]

5. Rake Sensitivity Modeling

The goal of the tool is to find the "Stall" or "Stability" window by mapping AB against the Rake Angle:

\[\text{Rake Angle } (\alpha) = \arctan\left(\frac{h_{rear} - h_{front}}{\text{Wheelbase}}\right)\]
